What is a Network?

A network is a collection of computers connected together through wires or wirelessely for sharing of resources and information. Both hardware and software are necessary to form a network. Networks work on the principles of Network Protocols. And there are many classifications of Computer Networks.

Classifications of Computer Networks

Computer Networks are classified into many types but I will discuss about two only, LAN and WAN.

LAN or Local Area Network

LAN short for Local Area Network is a type of Computer Network where computers or other devices connected with each other are within a limited area like the computers at an Office are connected with each other are an example of Local Area Network.

WAN or Wide Area Network

WAN short for Wide Area Network is a type of computer netwrok where devices connected to form a network are extended over a wide geographical area. Internet is the most famous example of the WAN where billions of devices are connected with each other to share data and information.

Network Topology

Networks are designed in many ways depending on the location or the budgest the network can use one of the Network Topologies to connect all the computers together. For example in a Star Topology layout, all the computers are connected with a single computer to form a network.

Communication Protocols

The Communications Protocols are set of rules for exchange of information over a network. Some examples of commonly used protocols are Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP), IEEE 802 and Ethernet etc.

Hardware and Software

Hardware and Software both are necessary for a computer network to work. Network cables, Access Points, Modems and Router are some devices that form the network plus a network operating systems are required for the networks to function properly.

The Computer Networks are what caused the World Wide Web to emerge into becoming what it is today and society of today cannot function without the use of web and the computer networks.
